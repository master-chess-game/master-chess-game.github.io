About Master Chess Online

Master Chess Online is an engaging digital version of the classic board game chess, available to play directly in your web browser. Developed by Code This Lab and released in March 2018, this game offers both single-player and multiplayer options, making it perfect for chess enthusiasts of all levels.

The game features three main modes: playing against an AI opponent with adjustable difficulty levels (easy, medium, hard), competing against a friend on the same device, or challenging players from around the world in online multiplayer matches. With a swift matchmaking system and timed turns, Master Chess keeps the excitement high while testing your strategic skills.

Master Chess has received high praise, boasting an 8.4 rating based on over 8,000 votes on platforms like CrazyGames. It's playable on desktop, mobile, and tablet, ensuring you can enjoy a game of chess anytime, anywhere.

How to Play Master Chess Online

Getting started with Master Chess is simple and intuitive. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Access the Game: Visit a platform like CrazyGames.com and search for "Master Chess" or go directly to the game page.
  2. Choose Your Mode: Select from AI opponent, local multiplayer (same device), or online multiplayer.
  3. Set Difficulty (for AI): If playing against the computer, choose easy for beginners, medium for intermediate, or hard for a challenge.
  4. Controls: Use your mouse or touch screen to select a piece (it will highlight possible moves) and click on the desired square to move.
  5. Timed Turns: Be quick! Turns are timed, adding pressure to your decisions.
  6. Win the Game: Checkmate your opponent's king to win. The game ends when a king is in check and cannot escape.

For beginners, start with no time limit options if available, and practice against the easy AI to learn the ropes.

Rules of Master Chess Online

Master Chess follows the standard rules of chess, with some digital enhancements for ease of play. Here's a breakdown:

  • Board Setup: An 8x8 grid with alternating black and white squares. White pieces start on rows 1-2, black on 7-8.
  • Pieces and Movements:
    • King: Moves one square in any direction.
    • Queen: Any number of squares vertically, horizontally, or diagonally.
    • Rook: Any number horizontally or vertically.
    • Bishop: Any number diagonally.
    • Knight: L-shape (2 in one direction, 1 perpendicular).
    • Pawn: Forward one square (two on first move), captures diagonally.
  • Special Moves: Castling (king and rook swap), en passant (pawn capture), pawn promotion (to queen or other piece upon reaching opposite side).
  • Check and Checkmate: King in threat is check; unable to escape is checkmate.
  • Draws: Stalemate, insufficient material, or threefold repetition.
  • Game-Specific: In Master Chess, possible moves are highlighted, and online matches use a matchmaking system.

Always remember, white moves first, and players alternate turns.

Fun Facts About Master Chess and Chess in General

  • Chess originated in India around the 6th century as "Chaturanga."
  • There are more possible chess games than atoms in the observable universe (Shannon number: 10^120).
  • Master Chess's AI is designed to challenge players at various levels, simulating real opponents.
  • The longest chess game theoretically possible is 5,949 moves.
  • Chess improves cognitive skills like problem-solving, memory, and concentration.
  • In Master Chess, over 8,000 players have rated it highly, making it a popular choice for online chess.
  • The word "checkmate" comes from the Persian "shah mat," meaning "the king is dead."
